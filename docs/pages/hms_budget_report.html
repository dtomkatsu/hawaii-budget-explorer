About HUMAN SERVICES

This department manages public benefit programs including Medicaid (Med-QUEST), the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF), general assistance, and public housing. Child and adult protective services, in-community youth programs, and the Hawai ªi Youth Correctional Facility are also overseen by the department. The State Commission on the Status of Women and the Fatherhood Commission are attached to HMS. The department is supported by more federal dollars than any other because most of its programs are partially funded by the federal government. In FY 2025, the department received $5,467,164 in emergency funding. The money will be used to upgrade the computer system that helps manage food stamp (SNAP) benefits in Hawaii. The goal is to fix mistakes in how benefits are given out, avoid future penalties, and possibly save the state money by not having to pay the rest of a federal fine.
